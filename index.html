About Me

I am a statistics graduate from the University of Ibadan, Nigeria, graduating as the Best Graduating Student with a CGPA of 3.79/4.00 and First Class Honours. My work sits at the intersection of Bayesian inference, machine learning, and applied quantitative modelling.

My undergraduate thesis developed Convoluted Bayesian Reference Class Forecasting (CB–RCF) — a novel extension of Bayesian RCF that explicitly models uncertainty across overlapping reference classes, applied to U.S. cancer incidence forecasting under high-dimensional demographic heterogeneity.

I am particularly motivated to combine rigorous statistical reasoning with modern AI architectures to build scalable, high-impact solutions for health analytics, disaster risk modelling, and financial risk assessment — especially in data-scarce sub-Saharan African settings.

Thesis
Convoluted Bayesian Reference Class Forecasting: Statistical Properties and Applications to Cancer Incidence

Proposed Convoluted Bayesian Reference Class Forecasting (CB–RCF) to forecast cancer incidence rates under high-dimensional demographic heterogeneity. Applied PCA to reduce multicollinearity across age-, sex-, and race-stratified U.S. state-level data (n=51), retaining 71.8% variance in three components. Used Gaussian mixture modelling to derive latent reference classes with probabilistic memberships, enabling closed-form covariate-conditioned posteriors and Bayesian model averaging for final predictions. CB–RCF outperformed OLS and standard RCF benchmarks (MAE=10.24, RMSE=13.51, Bayes Risk=1034.39), with variance decomposition confirming that predictive uncertainty arises from both in-class and inter-class sources.
